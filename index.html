Abstract

Formula 1  is a sport know as the pinnacle of racing, however most peaople dont understand the significance of the egineering behind it.
This paper will explore the physics behind aerodynamic designs in modern F1 racing cars and how airflow is utilized to make the car as 
optimal as possible when taking sharp corners at dangerousily high speeds.

Introduction

  
Formula 1 is know as the epitome of racing in modern day motorsports. It consists of highspeed sharp turns that 
require the cars to be heavily optimized aerodynamicaly, to generate as much downforce as possible without sacrificing speed. 
Every second counts and so the manipulation of airflow around the vehicles heavily affect the speed and stability of the car 
which can be the difference between winning or losing. As a result of this teams invest millions to innovate new technology,
to push the limits and reach record breaking speeds
